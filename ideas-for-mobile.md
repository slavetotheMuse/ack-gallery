# Ideas for Mobile — ACK Gallery

## Main Catalog (Portrait)
- Motherboard sits at bottom ~20% height, PCB traces and pulsating lines still active
- Single collection artwork fills most of screen as preview card
- Swipe left/right to browse collections
- Collection name and count float above the card
- Candles flank motherboard at bottom-left and bottom-right, scaled down but still glowing

## Entering a Collection
- Double-tap collection card → zooms through motherboard port (same portal feel)
- Full-screen single artwork view inside collection
- Swipe left/right to move between pieces
- Wires simplified to single thin gold line along bottom edge connecting port indicator to motherboard
- Motherboard stays as thin strip at very bottom (like a status bar)
- Swipe up to exit back to catalog swipe view

## Individual Artwork / Focus Mode
- Tap artwork to go full-screen, title at bottom-left (dash-dot style)
- COA card slides up from bottom as a half-sheet (instead of docking from right)
- Info overlay becomes a bottom sheet — drag up to see primary sale, activity feed, etc.

## Wire Simplification
- Keep just one or two signature wires for visual identity (not the full web)
- Repulsion/breathing effects simplified to subtle scale pulse on active card
- Prioritize smooth performance on mobile GPUs

## Object Manager
- Bottom drawer or hamburger menu that slides collection list in from the left
- Same C4D styling but touch-optimized (bigger tap targets, swipe to dismiss)

## Open Questions
- Navigation gestures: swipe-between-artworks + swipe-up-to-exit? Or tap-edges to navigate?
- How much of the node-editor aesthetic to preserve vs. simplify for small screens?
